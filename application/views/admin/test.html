How important is to maintain the visual hierarchy on the basis of Page scanning patterns?
Scanning patterns tend to take one of two shapes, “F” and “Z”. “F” pattern- A reader scans down the left side of the page, looking for interesting key words in left-aligned headings or initial topic sentences, then stopping and reading (to the right) when he or she comes to something interesting. “Z” pattern-A reader’s eye first scans across the top of the page, where important information is likely to be found, then shoots down to the opposite corner at a diagonal and does the same thing across the lower part of the page.
